GAP 1 — The Private Healthcare Gap

What: Aarogya Setu 2.0 is built around government rails — PM-JAY wallets, Jan Aushadhi, government scheme discovery, ABDM-registered (mostly public) facilities. Why it matters: The Indian who pays ₹49/month for an app is exactly the Indian who chooses a private clinic over a crowded government hospital. The government app structurally cannot become the marketplace for private doctors, private labs and private pharmacies — that would be the state competing with private business. How we win: MedRed becomes the layer for private healthcare: verified private doctor listings, private lab bookings, private hospital partnerships, medicine delivery from local chemists.

GAP 2 — The Tier-2/3 Hospital Technology Gap

What: Medanta runs its own patient app (UHID skip-the-queue, live lab report tracking, video consults, family linking) plus a doctor-side EMR app. That system took a corporate hospital chain years and crores to build. Why it matters: Thousands of good 50–200 bed private hospitals in Tier-2/3 cities have NOTHING — paper files, phone-call appointments, patients carrying report folders. How we win: We white-label MedRed as their hospital app. They get Medanta-level tech for a monthly fee; we get their patients into our ecosystem. Full plan in the Hospital OS tab.

GAP 3 — The Voice + WhatsApp + Trust Gap

What: The government app is app-first, form-first, literate-user-first — and early reviews already complain about lag and poor offline behaviour. Why it matters: Grandma doesn't download apps. She uses WhatsApp. She doesn't read — she listens. How we win: Report photo in on WhatsApp → Hindi voice note out. And for ₹49, a real registered doctor confirms it. "Doctor ne bola" beats "app ne dikhaya" — every single time in an Indian household.

What we DON'T fight — we ride the rails

ABHA / ABDM is infrastructure, like UPI. Nobody remembers NPCI — everyone uses PhonePe. We integrate ABHA record-pull so the government's 100-crore linked records become OUR data source. Position: the PhonePe of Indian health — private, delightful, on government rails.

The second business hiding inside the first

Hospital OS — Medanta-level tech for Tier-2/3 India

What Medanta built for itself, we rent to everyone else. Here's exactly what Medanta runs today, why smaller hospitals can't copy it, and how our version works.

What Medanta actually runs (researched, July 2026)

  • Patient app: book across 30+ specialties, video consults, UHID auto-creation to skip registration queues, all records (prescriptions, reports, discharge notes) in-app, LIVE lab & radiology report tracking, family member linking, order medicines from their pharmacy, home care booking, one-tap ambulance, preventive health packages.
  • Doctor app (Medanta EMR): doctors access patient EMR from their phone, place orders, refer across specialties, coordinate with ground teams.
  • The catch: it only works inside Medanta's own network — and it took a corporate chain years + crores to build.

The MedRed Hospital OS offer — what / how / when / why

WHAT the hospital gets: their own branded patient app (white-label MedRed) — skip-queue registration, appointment booking, auto report push with patient notification, live report status, and Doctor Copilot for their physicians.

HOW it works: hospital lab/reception uploads once → our pipeline OCRs, analyses, files to the right patient, and notifies them on app + WhatsApp. Doctors scan the patient's QR at the desk → full history in 30 seconds.

WHY hospitals say yes: zero dev cost, patients stop calling for reports, they look as modern as Medanta, and patient loyalty data stays visible to them.

WHY WE really do it: every partner hospital funnels its entire patient base into MedRed accounts — B2B revenue AND free B2C acquisition in one move.

WHEN: pilot with 2 hospitals in Shimla/Chandigarh in Phase 2 (months 4–6); productize in Phase 3.

Honest constraints — read before selling

  • Hospital sales are slow. Expect 2–3 months per close. That's why it's Phase 2/3, not V1.
  • Data agreements matter. Patient consent + DPDP-compliant contracts with each hospital, drafted once, reused everywhere.
  • Don't build custom per hospital. One product, their logo, their colours — nothing else changes. Custom = death for a 4-person team.
